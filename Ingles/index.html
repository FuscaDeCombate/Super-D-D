Dungeons and Dragons

Dungeons & Dragons is a high fantasy role-playing game where players create characters who embark on imaginary adventures in which they face monsters, gather treasure, interact with each other and gain experience points to become incredibly powerful as they the game progresses. The game's extensive rules, which cover areas such as social interactions, use of magic, combat and the effect of the environment on the characters, help the master in your decisions. The DM can choose which published rules he will use and even create new rules if necessary.

How to start your journey

The Starter Kit: Dragons of Storm Island is your gateway to action-packed adventures in the shared narrative game Dungeons & Dragons, where heroes face monsters, encounter treasures and participate in epic quests. This box contains the basic rules of the game plus everything you need to play with heroic characters involved in an ancient war between dragons as they explore the secrets of Storm Island.

This box contains the essentials you need to play a game of D&D with a Dungeon Master and one to five adventurers. A newly designed rulebook integrates players, teaching them how to create characters, and the included adventure, Dragon of Icespire Peak, features a new variant of 1 vs 1 rules.

D&D Player's Handbook is the essential book for anyone starting out in the world of Dungeons & Dragons. It is the main reference for every participant at a Dungeons & Dragons table. O book contains the rules for creating characters, backgrounds and skills, in addition to the rules for leveling up, exploration, combat and more. Use this guide to create characters from the races and most iconic D&D classes.

Among these books, many are ready-made adventures, the focus is on the text. Ready-made adventures are adventures, stories or scenarios ready to be used and that require little work for the master; since you already have the story, hook, characters, etc. This way the GM doesn't need to create the story from scratch, but still needs to manage the world, if adapt the players' decisions and, of course, make the 'scenes' flow. Think of ready-made adventures as topics of a slide presentation, where the master will have to take these topics, develop them and create nexus between them.
